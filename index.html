Overview on Violence in NYC Schools

Violence in schools is a long standing issue. Violence often stems from students' lack of emotional control resulting in escalation and subsequently violence. Students then face consequences that don’t aim to modify behavior, but instead push students further into violent tendencies and eventually further involvement with the criminal justice system. Our mission is to spread awareness about school violence and push for more mediation practices in schools over corporal punishment.

The harms of violence

  • Students in violent schools often feel unsafe, anxious and often perform worse in their academics (Lacoe, 2016).
  • Factors such as bullying, mental health struggles, discrimination, social isolation, and access to weapons contribute to violent behavior in schools, especially after the stress and isolation caused by the COVID-19 pandemic (Worrell, 2024).
  • School violence has increased in recent decades, including incidents such as school shootings. For example, 93 school shootings with casualties were reported in the 2020–2021 school year (American Institutes for Research, 2025).
  • In the 2018-2019 school year, there were 11,504. The increase comes amid debate over how schools should respond to discipline problems (Mokam, 2024). Though there is more awareness on violence in nyc schools, the lack of action to regulate it is proving problematic.

There have been many instances of physical and emotional violence. School shootings and fist fights have been major disrupters of education. With the age of technology, we also saw violence through cyberbullying. Violence leads to injury, death, trauma, anxiety, trust issues and poor performance. It is crucial to provide a safe community for students to learn as they are the future and education will open more doors for them.

Efforts to Reduce Violence

  • Government policies and programs, such as the Gun-Free Schools Act and the Dignity Act, attempt to reduce violence through funding, security improvements, and training (New York State Education Department, 2025).
  • Community organizations like the Kings Against Violence Initiative (KAVI) work to prevent violence by providing safe spaces, counseling, and conflict-resolution programs for students (KAVI, 2023).
  • By placing an emphasis on communication, the students gain a sense of support and protection and also have an opportunity to reflect and consider taking steps away from violence which can be prevented by reaching out for help in schools where free counseling is offered and by contacting a trusted adult when needed.

Researchers continue to debate whether government policies or parental involvement play the larger role in preventing violence in schools, highlighting an important gap in understanding effective solutions. By fostering a more organized and engaged community with the government and parents, there is a greater chance that violence in schools can be diminished.
